Project Overview

• Heart disease remains a leading cause of global mortality. While hospitals and insurance providers possess vast amounts of patient data, much of it remains underutilized, leading to reactive rather than proactive healthcare.

• This project leverages machine learning to analyze demographic, lifestyle, and clinical markers to predict the likelihood of a heart attack, enabling healthcare providers to deliver personalized, life-saving interventions before symptoms become critical.

Project Objectives

Predictive Assessment: Develop a classification model ($0/1$) to identify heart attack risk using medical and lifestyle data for early detection.
Holistic Analysis: Build a multi-factor model that captures the combined impact of demographic and clinical attributes for higher reliability.
Risk Stratification: Rank key variables (e.g., cholesterol, age, habits) to pinpoint the most significant contributors to cardiovascular risk.
